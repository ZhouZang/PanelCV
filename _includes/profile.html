My undergraduating thesis focuses on the impact of PM2.5 on infectious diseases in China. On finishing this dissertation, I realized how important an accurate PM2.5 dataset was and decided to further study the remote sensing of atmospheric aerosols.

My major work now is about atmospheric remote sensing and analyzing near surface pollution (PM2.5 and O3) and aerosol component (fine mode fraction) by satellite data and deep learning methods.

Main work and interest
atmospheric remote sensing

Reconstructing and near-real time monitoring of aerosols and near surface pollutant at global or national scale.

deep learning

New deep learning models that can help improve both accuracy and interpretability in satellite retrievals.

the spatial analysis of aerosols

The spatiotemporal characteristics of aerosols in recent decades and the possible drivers behind them.
